TallyKeep wireframes — v6

Three-act onboarding (Setup → Consolidation → Optimization). The deciding question is now hosted vs self-hosted, asked upfront. The Holding wizard is a multi-add experience focused on bringing the user's full Bitcoin position into one view ("UBS your fortune" framing).
Changelog vs v5:
  • New step 2: Hosted vs self-hosted choice with honest trade-offs surfaced
  • Encryption screen gains a biometric toggle (passphrase still required at setup; biometric for daily unlock)
  • New step 4 alternative: Hosted welcome screen (instead of node connection) for hosted-tier users
  • Step 5 reframed: "Let's bring everything together" with running tally; multi-add experience instead of single-Holding wizard
  • New contextual prompt: Auto-sweep prompt fires after first Account is added (mirrors the privacy prompt for Strongbox)
See spec_amendments.md for rationale.

Step 2 — Hosted or self-hosted?
The pivotal question. Two cards, side-by-side, identical structure for honest comparison. Trade-offs surfaced explicitly: self-hosted is harder but perfectly private; hosted is easier but we see metadata. Hosted greyed-out in v1 with "Coming soon" — see status table in spec_amendments.md.

Step 3 — Encryption + biometric toggle
Passphrase still required for setup and recovery — that's the cryptographic anchor. Biometric is an optional convenience for daily unlock thereafter, on by default if available. The OS-stored encryption key is what biometric unlocks; the seed/keys for Bitcoin remain untouched as always. WebAuthn-backed; works in modern browsers + Capacitor wrappers.

Privacy notice — read this
Because we run the backend, we see things you'd otherwise keep on your machine: your wallet descriptors (xpubs), the addresses TallyKeep watches, your transaction labels and categorizations, and your balance over time.

We never see your Bitcoin keys, signing material, or anything that lets us spend your funds. That stays on your hardware wallet (or your phone, for Lightning). Our threat model is in the docs — read it before you commit money.
I understand the privacy trade-off
Step 4 (hosted variant) — Welcome to your hosted instance
Replaces the node-connection step for users who chose hosted. The privacy notice is loud and explicit — we don't bury the trade-off in a tooltip. Toggle confirms acknowledgement before continuing. The "Switch to self-hosted" exit is honest about reversibility. v1 won't reach this screen because hosted is greyed; included here for the full vision.

One quick suggestion
Money sitting on an exchange is at the exchange's risk — not yours. Auto-sweep moves balance from Kraken to a wallet you control on a schedule or threshold. The exchange holds it briefly between trades; you hold the rest of the time.

Set up an auto-sweep now? You'll need a destination Holding (Strongbox or Vault) — we can use one you already have or help you set one up.
Auto-sweep contextual prompt — first Account added
Mirrors the privacy prompt from v5 (which fires on first Strongbox). Surfaces auto-sweep at the moment the user has just connected an exchange and the suggestion is concretely relevant — they have funds at custody risk, here's the mitigation. Three honest options: yes, maybe later, don't ask again.
