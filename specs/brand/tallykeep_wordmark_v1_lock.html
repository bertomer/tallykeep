Wordmark

The TallyKeep wordmark system. Two variants share a single typeface, a context-driven rule of use, and a sizing system covering display and default tiers plus a typography fallback for small UI. Decisions here are intentional and locked — not exploratory.

1 · Decisions, locked

The wordmark exists in two variants of the same brand name, used in different contexts. The choice between them is determined by context, not by user preference.

Two variants

  • Plain wordmark — tallykeep in Manrope 600 lowercase, no embedded mark.
  • Icon-y wordmark — tall + carved Y form (the canonical brand mark, without its cream container) + keep, all in Manrope 600 lowercase.

Rule of use

  • Plain wordmark — used when an icon-on-cream is already visible on the same surface (app icon corner, favicon tab, header lockup, splash screen). Embedding the icon-y here would make the brand mark appear twice.
  • Icon-y wordmark — used when the wordmark stands alone (footer, email signature, social bio, embedded inline references in articles, app store description, partner co-branding). The brand mark comes through the wordline itself.

Casing

  • tallykeep (lowercase) — wordmark only, in either variant.
  • TallyKeep (Title Case) — everything else: body text, app store name, email subject, code identifiers, filenames, formal documents.

Considered and dropped

  • Heritage-warm direction (Bricolage Grotesque, characterful display sans) — too craft-coded for the banking-grade brief; reads "indie / artisanal" rather than "trustworthy financial institution."
  • Abstracted custom y letterform (B2 in the exploration) — removed the matched-halves geometry that is the entire point of the brand mark; reduced to a generic "y with slanted descender" with no semantic load.
  • Camel-case wordmark as primary — body text reads weird in lowercase ("welcome to tallykeep" vs "Welcome to TallyKeep"). The split between lowercase wordmark and Title Case body is the fintech-standard convention (Wise, Qonto, Lemon, Ualá, Mailchimp).

2 · Typeface · Manrope 600

Manrope is a contemporary geometric sans designed for screen reading by Mikhail Sharanda. Free under the SIL Open Font License. Loaded via Google Fonts (no self-hosting required).

TallyKeep

Specs

  • Family: Manrope
  • Weight: 600 (semi-bold)
  • Letter-spacing: -0.022em (≈ −2 in 1000-em units) — gentle negative tracking, modern fintech default
  • Color: dark #1A1A1A on light backgrounds; cream #F4EAD5 on dark (carries from icon spec)
  • Loaded as: "Manrope", system-ui, sans-serif with system fallback

Why Manrope vs alternatives

  • vs DM Sans — cleaner terminals, less humanist warmth; better fit with the banking register.
  • vs Inter — less ubiquitous, gives the brand some category differentiation while staying in the safe modern-fintech zone.
  • vs Geist / Onest — same screen-optimization quality, slightly more presence in semi-bold and bolder weights, which matters because the icon's stick widths are matched to typographic stem weight at this rendering.
  • vs paid options (Söhne, Suisse, GT America) — equivalent quality, sustainable for a solo project, no license entanglement on the marketing site.

The font is rendered at weight 600 because the icon's stick widths (which are matched to the typographic stem weight at the locked sizing rule) only feel like part of the same wordline at this weight. Lighter weights leave the icon visually heavier than the typography; heavier weights leave it visually lighter.

3 · Variant 1 · plain wordmark

The default wordmark variant. Used in lockup contexts where the icon-on-cream is already visible on the same surface — app icon top-left of a header, favicon in the browser tab, splash screens, hero lockups that pair mark + wordmark, partner co-marketing tiles.

Default tier — 48 px font

Default · plainManrope 600 · font-size 48 · letter-spacing −1 · color #1A1A1A

Sizing rule

  • Any size from 14 px to 200 px works as plain Manrope 600 lowercase.
  • Letter-spacing scales with font-size: roughly letter-spacing ≈ −0.022 × font-size in pixels (= −2 at 92 px, −1 at 48 px, etc.).
  • No minimum size for the plain variant — it's just type. Below ~14 px, switch to weight 700 for legibility.

Color modes

Light mode #1A1A1A on cream / white
Dark mode #F4EAD5 on dark navy / black

4 · Variant 2 · icon-y wordmark

The standalone wordmark variant. Used when the wordmark stands alone and the brand mark needs to come through the wordline itself — footer signatures, email signatures, social bios, embedded inline text in articles, app store descriptions, partner co-branding tiles.

The icon used is the canonical icon-on-cream form (see tallykeep_brand_mark_v1_lock) without its cream container. The carved Y sits on its own; the V-notches and hanging hole are filled with the page background colour so they read as knockouts.

The icon used inside the wordmark

Same canonical icon as the brand mark spec, with two adjustments for in-wordmark use:

  • No cream container — the carved Y sits on its own. The cream rounded square is reserved for square-format contexts (favicon, app icon, social avatar) where the brand mark needs to be self-contained.
  • Notches and hanging hole knockouts are filled with the page background colour (typically #FAFAF7 or the colour of the wordmark surface) so they punch through cleanly. Without a cream substrate, the canonical cream fills would be visible bumps; the page-bg fill makes them read as carved-through.

Cropping in the SVG: a nested <svg viewBox="36.43 27.79 131.9 146.43"> isolates the icon's visible bounding box (excludes the empty corners of the canonical 200×200 viewBox), so the y-glyph's footprint in the wordline is the actual Y, not a square containing it. Don't change this inner viewBox — it's geometry, not a tweakable parameter.

5 · Sizing system & formulas

Two locked tiers cover most display contexts. For sizes between or outside the locked tiers, use the linear formulas — they reproduce the locked values within ±1 px and provide a defensible design rule for any font-size in the supported range.

Useful range: fs = 24 to 120. Outside this, hand-tune in the playground (§4) and verify visually. The non-zero constants (+8.7, +8.5, −5.5) carry real design intent: at small font sizes, decorative elements need slightly more relative weight than a pure proportional rule would give, otherwise stick widths drop below the legibility threshold.

Stick-width threshold

The icon's stick width at any rendered size is 22 × (icon_size / 146.43). The minimum legible stick width is ~5 px. Below this, switch to the plain wordmark variant — do not render the icon-y at smaller sizes; it produces visual noise rather than a brand mark.

The threshold puts icon-y minimum at:

  • icon_size ≥ 33 px to keep sticks above 5 px
  • fs ≥ 30 px (using the icon_size formula) to satisfy that constraint
  • In practice: round to fs ≥ 24 (default tier minimum) for the icon-y variant.

Letter-spacing

Letter-spacing scales linearly with font-size: letter-spacing ≈ −0.022 × fs (in px).

  • fs=92 → letter-spacing −2
  • fs=48 → letter-spacing −1
  • fs=24 → letter-spacing −0.5 (or 0; sub-pixel tracking is noisy at small sizes)

TK monogram — for ultra-small UI

Below ~16 px height, even the plain wordmark loses readability. For circular avatars, tiny status badges, and dense UI dots, a TK monogram serves as the brand-mark substitute. Capital letters in Manrope 600, dark text on cream container (matches the brand mark's substrate). The container is provided by the consuming context (avatar circle, badge); the monogram itself is just two letters.

Two forms of the brand name exist. They are not interchangeable.

Examples

  • ✓"TallyKeep is a self-hosted Bitcoin banking app for Argentina, Africa, and the EU."
  • ✗"tallykeep is a self-hosted Bitcoin banking app..." — reads as a typo or low-effort indie startup.
  • ✓Web header: [wordmark: tallykeep] visual, body copy says "TallyKeep"
  • ✗Web header: body text "TallyKeep" with no visual wordmark — use the wordmark.
  • ✓Email subject: "Welcome to TallyKeep"
  • ✗Email subject: "Welcome to tallykeep"
  • ✓Code: const tk = new TallyKeep()
  • ✓Repo: github.com/remy/tallykeep (lowercase fine in URLs)

The pattern follows fintech convention: Wise, Qonto, Lemon, Ualá, Mailchimp, Stripe all reserve their lowercase form for the visual wordmark and use Title Case in everything written. Lowercase in body copy reads as informal / unbranded; Title Case carries the institutional register the brand is positioning for.

Dark-mode color rules

  • Wordmark text: #F4EAD5 (cream)
  • Icon wood: #A88554 (same oak as light mode — brand-color consistency, passes WCAG AA)
  • Icon grain: #6F5638 (same as light mode — darker brown reads as shadow on the wood)
  • Notches and hanging hole: #1A1A1A (page bg — same knockout principle as light mode, just inverted)
  • Page background: #1A1A1A standard; the surface can be darker (e.g. #0E0E0E) but the icon's knockouts must match whichever surface the wordmark sits on.

8 · Don'ts

Don't use the icon-y wordmark when an icon-on-cream is also visible on the same surface. The brand mark would appear twice. Use the plain wordmark instead.
Don't write tallykeep in body text. Lowercase belongs to the visual wordmark only. Body copy uses TallyKeep.
Don't use TALLYKEEP all-caps anywhere. All-caps reads as shouting / corporate-from-the-90s.
Don't substitute a different y at small sizes. Below the icon-y minimum (~24 px), fall back to the plain wordmark — not a custom or stylized y.
Don't change Manrope's letter-spacing beyond the formula in §5. Tighter or looser tracking breaks the wordmark's tested visual rhythm.
Don't change the icon's geometry for any reason — cropping viewBox, biais cut, notch positions, hanging hole. The icon spec is locked.
Don't recolor the wordmark text. Use only the locked color values (#1A1A1A or #F4EAD5).
Don't stretch, skew, rotate, or otherwise distort either variant. No vertical compression to fit narrow spaces; no horizontal stretching.
Don't pair the icon-y wordmark with a tagline directly underneath — the visual gets crowded. If a tagline is needed, use the plain wordmark + tagline lockup.
Don't put the wordmark on a background that drops contrast below 4.5:1. WCAG AA minimum.
Don't use weights other than 600. Lighter or heavier breaks the icon-stick / typographic-stem weight balance.

9 · Tagline · in consideration

Not locked. Candidates explored, top picks held for real-user testing before commit. The tagline lives alongside the wordmark in marketing surfaces (hero pages, app-store description, social bios, conference signage); the wordmark stands alone in product UI, so the tagline isn't a hard dependency — it can be added later without breaking anything else in the brand.

What the tagline is compressing

Every digital asset other than Bitcoin is a bet on a counterparty that can default — fiat on a state's solvency, equity on a company's, stablecoins on a custodian's, even other crypto-assets on a foundation's continued operation. Bitcoin's only structural failure mode is that the internet stops existing. The world currently uses BTC as a leveraged trade against fiat (sell when EUR-price is high), when the structurally correct use is to hold and spend it as money. The tools for that use don't exist. TallyKeep is the missing tool.

The tagline doesn't need to make this whole argument — it just needs to point at the conclusion (use Bitcoin as money, not as a bet) without forcing the reader to agree with the full thesis on first impression. That keeps the door open for users who haven't arrived at fiat-defaultism independently.

Candidates

CandidateJobNotes
Banking, not betting Promise + behavior contrast Top pick. Three words. Contrasts what TallyKeep enables (use as money) vs. dominant misuse (trade for fiat). Doesn't require the reader to share the full thesis — it's a behavior shift, not a doctrinal claim.
Stop betting. Start banking. Promise + assertive call Same idea, more swagger. Risk: imposes the thesis on the user ("you've all been doing it wrong"). Founder concern that this contradicts measured personal voice.
Bitcoin, used as money Descriptor Factual, App-Store-ready. Useful as the formal category line in store listings and PSAN/DASP filings. Not the primary marketing tagline.
Money without a counterparty Positioning Captures the structural argument sharply. Risk: "counterparty" is jargon outside finance; assumes a converted audience.
From their hands to yours Custody contrast Surfaced 2026-05-13 during the Add Holding picker design pass — considered for the picker sub-heading but rejected there because the spectrum shifts axis at type 2 (Account→Purse = custody transfer; Purse→Strongbox→Vault = exposure/ceremony, not "more yours"). Holds as a marketing-register tagline because the brand surface works at the high-level pitch where the linear-progression nuance doesn't matter. 5 words, banking-grade voice, no maxi vocabulary, doesn't require the fiat-defaultism thesis to land. Reads alongside "Banking, not betting" — different axis (custody vs use-pattern), both viable in different contexts (about page / hero / app-store description).
Bitcoin, as intended Promise + philosophical Earlier candidate. Carries the Satoshi-vision claim without the trade-vs-money explicit framing. Works if you want the historical-philosophical angle instead of the behavior contrast.
Bitcoin, finally Promise (gut version) Founder's gut pull. Tightest, most confident, most exposed. Less anchored than "Banking, not betting" — claims confidence with no specific referent.

Strategic caution

  • A tagline is an INVITATION to the thesis, not the thesis itself. The fiat-defaultism argument belongs in long-form copy, the about page, conference talks — not the headline.
  • Founder voice asymmetry: the brand can be confident even when the founder is measured (Wise's "Money without borders" is bold; the founders are quiet engineers). A bold tagline is available even if the founder is not naturally bold.
  • The brand mark + product context carries part of the meaning. If "Bitcoin" is implicit through the surrounding surface (the icon-y wordmark, the about-page copy), the tagline can drop "Bitcoin" entirely and focus on the banking/intent claim — unlocks shorter forms like "Banking. As intended."

Before locking

Tagline candidates require real-user testing before commit — say the candidates out loud to two or three target users (Argentine non-technical BTC user, EU general consumer, African remittance sender) and observe what they recognize. If reaction to "Banking, not betting" is "yeah, that's what I want," it's the tagline. If it's "I don't get it," tune.

10 · Open items (deferred)

Items not in this lock; to be addressed in future iterations.

Trademark check
EUIPO classes 9 (software) and 36 (financial services); INPI in Argentina. Required before public announcement, regardless of distribution channel. Carried over from the icon spec's open list — the wordmark and the icon are checked together as the brand combination.
Cyrillic / non-Latin script support
Manrope ships with Latin Extended and Cyrillic. Current target markets (Argentina, French, English, Portuguese, Swahili) are all Latin-script. Lower priority — likely post-public-shipment, only re-validated if Russia, MENA, or East Asia enter scope.
Print specifications · side note
Parked. CMYK + Pantone for the wood colours, paper guidance, minimum print sizes — only relevant if/when business cards, conference swag, or printed pitch decks actually happen. TallyKeep is digital-first; not on the v1 path.
