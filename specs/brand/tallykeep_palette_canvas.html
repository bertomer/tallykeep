8 · Dark mode

not defined yet — placeholder
Dark-mode tokens are not defined in tokens.css yet. Sections 4 and 1 above use --color-text as the dark surface (a hack to get a "dark on light" preview). A proper dark mode needs its own surface ramp.
  • Required new tokens: --color-bg-dark, --color-surface-dark, --color-surface-raised-dark, --color-border-dark, --color-text-dark, --color-text-muted-dark.
  • Brand-mark carving on dark — solved. Any wrapper carrying the .on-dark-surface class re-scopes --brand-mark-carving to the dark ink locally. Pattern is in tokens.css. When dark-mode page surfaces ship, the same class applies to <body> (or the dark-mode root) and the carvings track automatically — no new token required.
  • Semantic states need a dark variant — particularly the soft backgrounds, which currently use pale tints unsuited to dark surfaces.

9 · Tensions in the current palette

visible problems · resolve before locking
Holding-account vs brand-mark wood

The Account-type accent (--color-holding-account, limestone) and the brand-mark wood (--brand-mark-wood) are visually nearly identical when the wood leans olive-tan. On a screen with the wordmark in the app bar and an Account holding in the list, the two read as the same material.

account limestone mark wood

Decision needed: retune limestone (palette v2 → v3), or shift the wood away from limestone's hue.

Primary vs success — both green

--color-primary (verdigris) and --color-success (holly leaf) are both deep saturated greens. On a screen showing a primary CTA and a success banner together, the user may not parse "brand" vs "this means success" at a glance. Hue gap is small (~30°).

primary verdigris success holly

Decision needed: shift success toward a yellower / brighter green to widen the hue gap, or commit to the collision (success only ever appears far from a CTA).

Brand mark at favicon size

Look at the 16px solid icon in §1. The wood-and-grain relationship breaks down at sub-pixel scales — grain becomes noise, then disappears. With low-saturation woods (the olive-tan), the silhouette flattens further. The mark v1 lock accounts for this by providing a solid-only variant for ≤16px, but the solid variant is still low-contrast at that scale.

Decision needed: either widen the wood/grain luminance gap, or accept that the favicon reads as a flat tan shape (banking on the "Y silhouette" alone for recognition).

Identity SVGs vs canvas inlines

brand/identity/icon-canonical.svg, icon-solid.svg, and wordmark-icony.svg still hardcode brand v1 Aged Oak (#A88554) and Grain (#6F5638). Frontend imports of these will NOT track the palette — only inline SVGs with .mark-* class hooks (mockups + this canvas) follow tokens.css.

Decision needed at lock-doc bump: either rewrite the identity SVGs to use class-based fills (and document the "consumer must inline, not <img>" rule), or regenerate them with the current adopted hex (and accept that swaps require regeneration). The first is more flexible; the second matches brand/README.md brand → tokens lockstep more cleanly.
